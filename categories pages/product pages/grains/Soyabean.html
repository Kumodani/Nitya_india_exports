Description

Soybeans, scientifically known as Glycine max, originate from East Asia and are one of the most widely cultivated legumes in the world. Due to their rich texture, mild taste, and high protein content, they serve as an excellent meat alternative, making them a preferred choice for vegetarians and vegans.

    Health Benefits of Soybeans

  • Rich in Plant-Based Protein: An excellent source of complete protein, containing all essential amino acids.
  • Heart Health: Soy protein is known to help lower bad cholesterol (LDL) levels.
  • Bone Strength: High in calcium and isoflavones, which contribute to bone health.
  • Weight Management: A great option for a protein-rich, low-fat diet.
  • Hormonal Balance: Contains phytoestrogens that may support hormonal health, particularly in women.

      Uses and Benefits of Soyabean

  • Whole Beans: Used in curries, stews, and salads.
  • Soy Milk: A dairy-free milk alternative rich in protein.
  • Soy Flour: A high-protein gluten-free flour alternative.
  • Soy Chunks & Soya Chaap: Commonly used as a meat substitute in various dishes.
  • Soybean Oil: A widely used cooking oil.
  • Tofu & Tempeh: Plant-based protein sources derived from soybeans.
